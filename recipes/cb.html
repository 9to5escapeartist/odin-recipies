Maple de Crème Brûlée

Maple de Crème Brûlée

This is an exquisite, simple and highly impressive dish. If you have not made this before, you must give it a crack and share with someone you love.

Ingredients

  • 500ml double cream.
  • 100ml of the best quality full cream milk you can find.
  • 1 tea spoon of organic vanilla bean (If you can, get a real vanilla pod and scoop the inside out).
  • 5 large egg yolks.
  • 50ml granulated sugar.
  • Maple syrup (the best quality you can find, must be organic).

Cooking instructions

  1. Heat oven to 180°C
  2. Sit two deep ramekins in a deep roasting tin, at least 10cm deep.
  3. Slowly pour cream into a small to medium pan and with the milk.
  4. Slice the vanilla pod in half lengthsways, use the knife to scrape out the vanilla bean seeds into cream/milk mixture and incude the vanilla pod as well.
  5. Place egg yolks and sugar into mixing bowk and whisk for 1 minute with an electric mixer - until it has a thicker and fluffy form.
  6. Bring cream/milk mix to a boil on a medium heat - as soon as bubbles appear around the edge of the pot, take it off the heat.
  7. Pour hot cream mix into the egg mix and stir with wire whisk and ensure you scrape out the seeds from the pan.
  8. Pour hot mixture through a fine sieve thats sitting on top of a bowl / jug ect. Use a spoon to massage any vanilla seeds through mesh.
  9. Scoop all the pale foam sitting at the top of the mix, should be a few spoonfuls - dischard and stir the mix.
  10. Pour hot water in roasting tin. Enough that ramekins are submerged 1.5cm into water.
  11. Pour hot cream mix into ramekins - fill just below the top.
  12. Pop them into the oven with baking paper sheet over the top of the tin - loosely to allow airflow through.
  13. Bake for 30-35 mins, until mixture is set (should be like jelly) should wobble a bit, don't over bake and make them too firm.
  14. Take ramekins out of the roasting tin - use gloves and set them on a wire rack to cool for 4 mins, then transfer to the fridge.
  15. Take ramekins out of the fridge and sprinkle 1 and a half tsp of caster sugar on top.
  16. Use a little maple syrup to slightly dampen the sugar, then use a blow torch to caramelise it (best to keep the torch moving in small circles just above the sugar and maple syrup).
  17. Serve them up and eat within an hour.
